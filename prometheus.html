Prometheus Biography

Name: Prometheus
Lineage: He is a Titan, one of the pre-Olympian gods, born to the Titan Iapetus and the nymph Clymene or Asia. His brothers include Atlas, Epimetheus, and Menoetius.
Appearance: Prometheus being a titan is often depicted as having a human resemblance, however, specific details like hair color or facial features are not consistently described in ancient texts.
Personality: Prometheus is particularly known for his intelligence as he was the one entrusted by Zeus to create the humans on Earth. Because of this, he holds a deep empathy for humans, which leads to his defiance against the gods.
Legacy: Prometheus plays a crucial role in Greek mythology, especially concerning human beings whom he created in clay. His actions of stealing fire from the gods and giving it to humans allowed for the spread of knowledge and civilization.
